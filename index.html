Why This Matters

Small towns deserve big dreams too — especially when children need safe places to move, laugh, and make friends.

In bigger cities, playgrounds and activity spaces may feel common. In rural communities, those places are rare and precious. Ninja Park is a hopeful project that turns simple bottle collection into a shared way for families to build belonging together.

Every bottle collected is a small act that helps a local child feel welcomed, a family feel seen, and a community grow stronger.

Zipline Heart

The proposed zipline is the main attraction, emotional centerpiece, and first sponsorship focus of Ninja Park.

The zipline remains a future attraction concept only. It is the signature dream of the park design, not a current or approved play feature.

Zipline planning principles:

  • Professional zipline design and engineers must review the plan.
  • Certified installation, inspection, insurance, and permits are required before opening.
  • Ongoing maintenance, safety supervision, and accessibility review are essential.
  • No child may use the feature until all safety approvals, training, and documentation are in place.

Zipline sponsorship interest is prioritized while all project details remain subject to confirmed planning, review, and community approval.

Trust & Privacy

This website is an awareness and preview page. Official details will be added only after they are confirmed.

No personal information is collected on this website. There are no forms aimed at children, and the site is adult-facing only.

There are no active payment links on this preview site. When donation details are ready, they will be shared clearly and responsibly.

Child safety and ethics focus:

  • Children’s photos and stories require parent or guardian consent before sharing.
  • Volunteer support is based on supervision, privacy, screening where needed, and reporting safety concerns.
  • The park is not currently open, approved, certified, or ready for public use.
  • We will only share formal cultural guidance with appropriate local Indigenous Nations, Métis representatives, Inuit representatives, Elders, Knowledge Keepers, families, and community members.
